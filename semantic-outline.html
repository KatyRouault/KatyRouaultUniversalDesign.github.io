Overview

The monarch butterfly (Danaus plexippus) is one of the most recognizable and well-studied butterflies in the world. Known for its striking orange, black, and white pattern, it plays an important role in pollination and ecological balance.

Habitat and Migration

Monarchs are famous for their incredible migration, traveling thousands of miles from North America to central Mexico. During this migration, multiple generations are born and die before the final generation returns north.

Diet

Monarch caterpillars feed exclusively on milkweed, which provides them with toxins that make them distasteful to predators. Adult butterflies feed on nectar from various flowers.

Threats and Conservation

Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change have led to a decline in monarch populations. Conservation efforts focus on planting milkweed, protecting overwintering sites, and raising public awareness.

Did You Know?

  • Monarchs can travel up to 3,000 miles during migration.
  • They can only lay eggs on milkweed plants.
  • Their bright color warns predators of toxicity.
